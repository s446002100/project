Course Description

This course provides an in-depth introduction to 3D modelling and animation, equipping students with the technical and creative skills necessary to design and animate 3D assets. Students explore modelling, texturing, rigging, animation, and rendering using tools such as Autodesk Maya, Blender, and Unreal Engine, while aligning work with HCI principles for usability and engagement.

Main Objectives

  • Master 3D modeling, texturing, rigging, animation, and rendering to create high-quality assets.
  • Develop hands-on expertise in Autodesk Maya, Blender, and Unreal Engine.
  • Design assets for games, simulations, and interactive applications.
  • Align 3D assets with HCI principles to enhance usability and engagement.

Course Learning Outcomes

Category Learning Outcome
Knowledge & Understanding Understand the fundamental principles of 3D modelling and animation, including topology, rigging, and keyframe animation.
Skills Utilize industry-standard 3D modelling and animation tools (e.g., Autodesk Maya, Blender) to create assets.
Skills Develop realistic and stylized animations using keyframe and procedural techniques.
Skills Integrate 3D assets into game engines (e.g., Unreal Engine or Unity) for interactive experiences.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to 3D Modelling and Animation & Environment Setup
  2. Modelling Fundamentals (Topology and Edge Flow)
  3. Subdivision Modelling and Sculpting
  4. Texturing and UV Mapping
  5. Basics of Rigging: Bones, Joints, and Skinning
  6. Lighting and Rendering
  7. Procedural Animation Techniques
  8. Integration with Game Engines
  9. Principles of Environmental Design for Games and Simulations
  10. Facial Animation and Lip-syncing
  11. Optimization Techniques for 3D Assets
  12. Keyframes and Interpolation
  13. Autodesk Maya Hands-on
  14. Blender and Unity for 3D Assets
